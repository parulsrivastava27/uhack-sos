Agencies connected with us

The National Disaster Management Authority (NDMA) is a critical governmental organization tasked with disaster preparedness, mitigation, and management in India. Established in 2005, it operates under the aegis of the Ministry of Home Affairs. NDMA plays a pivotal role in formulating policies, coordinating efforts, and ensuring a comprehensive and effective response to natural and man-made disasters across the country. It works in collaboration with state-level disaster management authorities and various stakeholders to enhance disaster resilience, develop strategies, and provide guidance for disaster risk reduction. NDMA's mission is to create a safer and more resilient India by promoting a culture of disaster preparedness and proactive response measures.

National Disaster Response Force (NDRF)

NDRF is a specialized force dedicated to disaster response and rescue operations. It consists of multiple battalions strategically stationed across the country, ready to deploy in the event of disasters.

State Disaster Response Force (SDRF)

The SDRF is a specialized force established by individual states and union territories in India to respond to disasters and emergency situations at the state level. Each state and union territory has its own SDRF, which operates under the overall guidance and coordination of the National Disaster Response Force (NDRF) and the National Disaster Management Authority (NDMA).

Central Armed Police Forces (CAPFs)

CAPF stands for "Central Armed Police Forces," which is a collective term used to refer to various paramilitary forces and security organizations in India. These forces are under the jurisdiction of the Ministry of Home Affairs, Government of India, and they play a crucial role in maintaining internal security, assisting in disaster management, and providing support during emergencies.
